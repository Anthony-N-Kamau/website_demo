Dutch Forest Change Detection
Using NDVI Temporal Profiles

An MSc Applied Data Science term project for INFOMSSML – Spatial Statistics & Machine Learning (2026). We investigate how forest greenness has changed across Utrecht and Gelderland provinces between 2013 and 2025, using satellite-derived NDVI time series from Landsat 7, 8, and 9.

Research Question: To what extent do NDVI temporal profiles differ between forests within Utrecht and Gelderland, and did the rate or direction of change shift between the 2013–2017 and 2017–2021 periods?

Background

Dutch forests were subsidised from 1986 to address wood shortages. Since 2013, deforestation has outpaced planting — with forests converted to heathlands and other nature types.

Research Aim

Detect and quantify local variation in forest greenness over time, using NDVI change detection across two time windows: 2013–2017 and 2017–2021.

Study Area

Forests in the provinces of Utrecht and Gelderland, the Netherlands — two regions with significant documented forest loss since 2013.

Data

Landsat 7, 8, and 9 multispectral imagery sourced from Microsoft Planetary Computer, covering 2013 to 2025. Processed to derive NDVI time series for forest pixels.

Methods

NDVI temporal profiling with change detection across two periods. Spatial statistics and machine learning techniques applied to identify deforestation hotspots and trend shifts.

Expected Results

NDVI temporal profiles per forest zone, maps of deforested areas by time period, and comparative analysis of change rates between the two study windows.

Tools

Python (via Planetary Computer API), R for spatial statistics, QGIS for visualisation. Key packages: rioxarray, geopandas, spatialRF, sf, tmap.

Course Context

Term project for INFOMSSML (Spatial Statistics and Machine Learning), MSc Applied Data Science, Utrecht University. Supervised by Dr. SM Labib and Dr. Menno Straatsma.
