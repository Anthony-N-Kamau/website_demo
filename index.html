Introduction

Dutch forests have undergone significant change over the past decades. In 1986, the Dutch government subsidised large-scale tree planting to address a national wood shortage. Since 2013, however, this trend has reversed — deforestation has consistently outpaced new planting, with forested areas being converted to heathlands and other nature types.

This project investigates how and where these changes have occurred across the provinces of Utrecht and Gelderland — two regions with well-documented forest loss. By analysing satellite-derived NDVI (Normalised Difference Vegetation Index) time series, we aim to detect local variation in forest greenness and identify where change has accelerated or reversed across two time windows: 2013–2017 and 2017–2021.

This is the term project for INFOMSSML (Spatial Statistics and Machine Learning), MSc Applied Data Science, Utrecht University — supervised by Dr. SM Labib and Dr. Menno Straatsma.

Research

Research Aim

Find local variation between forests using NDVI time series and perform change detection across the study area. We compare forest greenness trends across two time periods to determine whether deforestation rates have shifted in direction or magnitude.

Research Question

To what extent do NDVI temporal profiles differ between forests within Utrecht and Gelderland, and did the rate or direction of change shift between the 2013–2017 and 2017–2021 periods?

Expected Results

  • Temporal NDVI profiles per forest zone across both provinces
  • Maps of deforested areas broken down by time period
  • Comparative analysis of NDVI change rates between the two study windows
  • NDVI imagery visualising greenness trends over time

Data & Methods

Study Area

Forests in the Dutch provinces of Utrecht and Gelderland, covering the period 2013 to 2025.

Data

  • Source: Microsoft Planetary Computer
  • Imagery: Landsat 7, 8, and 9 multispectral scenes
  • Derived variable: NDVI (Normalised Difference Vegetation Index) time series

Methods

  • NDVI computation from near-infrared and red spectral bands
  • Temporal profiling across 2013–2017 and 2017–2021
  • Change detection to identify deforestation hotspots
  • Spatial statistics and machine learning for pattern analysis

Tools & Software

  • Python — Planetary Computer API, rioxarray, geopandas, pystac
  • R — sf, tmap, spatialRF
  • QGIS — Spatial visualisation and map production
